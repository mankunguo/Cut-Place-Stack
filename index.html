Cut, Place, Stack
?
Cut Place Stacking is a performative design tool that investigates the intuitive role of the body in graphic surface production. By inviting participants to manipulate colored paper in front of a camera, the project transforms physical gestures into an immediate and labor-intensive process of producing typographic choreography. The tool foregrounds the body's centrality in design, turning every movement—cutting, placing, stacking—into a live visual outcome.
